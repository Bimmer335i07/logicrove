Inside the audit

You do not need another SEO report. You need to know why AI is choosing other firms over yours.

Six findings, one written verdict, a prioritized remediation list you can hand to any marketing team.

I.

Citation map across four AI surfaces

We run fifteen real prospect prompts through ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and Google AI Overviews. You learn which of your cases are surfacing, and which competitors are getting named instead.

II.

Share of voice versus your top five rivals

A plain English scoreboard. How often each firm is mentioned, in which contexts, with which language. We name the firms. You see the distance in black and white.

III.

Structured data and schema inventory

AI systems read your site through schema markup. Missing LegalService, Attorney, or Review schema is the most common reason firms do not appear. We list every gap, file by file.

IV.

E-E-A-T signal assessment

Experience, expertise, authority, trust. The signals AI uses to decide who to recommend. We grade each signal against the three firms ranking ahead of you, and show what they have that you do not.

V.

Citation source gap analysis

AI models cite a small pool of authoritative sources. We identify the directories, legal publications, and review sites that AI trusts in your jurisdiction, and flag the ones you are missing from.

VI.

Prioritized remediation plan

Every finding is scored by impact and effort. Fifteen to twenty items, ranked, with plain instructions. Your current marketing vendor can execute the list. No ongoing retainer with us required.

Common questions

What firms ask before commissioning the audit.

How is this different from an SEO audit?

An SEO audit measures how your site performs in traditional Google search: rankings, backlinks, page speed, keyword density. This audit measures something different entirely. It measures how AI systems like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews describe, recommend, and cite your firm when a prospective client asks for help. The ranking factors are different. The optimization strategies are different. Most SEO agencies are not yet equipped to diagnose AI visibility problems because the field is still in its early stages.

My firm already ranks well on Google. Do I still need this?

Google ranking and AI recommendation are two separate systems. A firm can hold the number one organic position for "personal injury lawyer in Houston" and still be absent from ChatGPT and Perplexity responses for the same query. AI models pull from different signals: structured data, E-E-A-T markers, citation sources, and schema markup that traditional SEO does not always address. The audit shows you whether your Google success is translating into AI visibility, or whether competitors are capturing that new channel while you assume you are covered.

They surface a handful of findings, enough to create urgency, then steer you toward a long term contract. Our audit is the complete deliverable. You receive the full picture and decide what to do with it on your own terms.

The Snapshot is a one to two page brief covering one city and five AI platforms across 25 prompts. Can my current marketing agency do this for me?

They can execute the recommendations. That is exactly what the remediation list is designed for. But most marketing agencies do not yet have the methodology to diagnose AI visibility gaps, because the tools and techniques are still emerging. The audit gives your agency a clear, prioritized list of what to fix. Think of it as a second opinion from a specialist before your general practitioner begins treatment.

What if AI recommends my firm already?

Then the audit confirms your position and shows you exactly why you are being recommended, so you can protect that advantage. It also identifies where competitors are gaining ground and where your visibility is weakest across different prompt types. Knowing you are ahead is useful. Knowing exactly where and by how much is actionable.
